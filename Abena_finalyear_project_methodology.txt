Technical Methodology for the AI-Powered Local Business Assistant
The methodology explains how we will build the system from the technical side.
The project will be built as a web-based AI customer support platform where a business owner uploads business information, and customers ask questions through a chatbot. The AI will answer using only the uploaded business data through Retrieval-Augmented Generation, also known as RAG.
________________________________________
1. System Overview
The system will have three main users:
1.	Business Owner
o	Adds business information.
o	Uploads products, services, prices, and FAQs.
o	Updates stock availability.
o	Views customer questions.
2.	Customer
o	Asks questions through a web chatbot or WhatsApp.
o	Receives automatic AI responses.
3.	System Admin
o	Manages businesses.
o	Monitors usage.
o	Handles abuse or system errors.
The basic flow is:
Business Owner uploads business data
        ↓
System processes and stores the data
        ↓
Customer asks a question
        ↓
System searches the business data
        ↓
AI generates an answer
        ↓
Customer receives response

4. RAG Methodology
This is the most important technical part of the project.
RAG stands for:
Retrieval-Augmented Generation
It means the AI does not just answer from its general knowledge. It first retrieves relevant information from the business’s uploaded data, then uses that information to generate an answer.
________________________________________
RAG Pipeline
Business Data Upload
        ↓
Data Cleaning
        ↓
Text Chunking
        ↓
Embedding Generation
        ↓
Vector Storage
        ↓
Customer Question
        ↓
Question Embedding
        ↓
Similarity Search
        ↓
Relevant Context Retrieval
        ↓
LLM Response Generation
        ↓
Final Answer
________________________________________
Step 1: Data Collection
The system collects business data from:
•	Product forms
•	Service forms
•	FAQ forms
•	Uploaded CSV files
•	Uploaded PDF files
•	Business profile information
All these will be converted into clean text.
Example raw product data:
HP EliteBook 840 G6, GHS 4,200, Available, Core i5, 8GB RAM, 256GB SSD
Converted text:
Product: HP EliteBook 840 G6.
Category: Laptop.
Price: GHS 4,200.
Availability: Available.
Specification: Core i5, 8GB RAM, 256GB SSD.
Warranty: 3 months.
________________________________________
Step 2: Data Cleaning
Before sending data to the AI system, the text must be cleaned.
Cleaning includes:
•	Removing empty spaces
•	Removing duplicate information
•	Fixing broken text from PDFs
•	Standardizing price format
•	Standardizing availability status
•	Removing irrelevant characters
Example:
HP laptop!!!! price--4200gh
Cleaned version:
HP laptop price is GHS 4,200.
________________________________________
Step 3: Text Chunking
Large documents will be split into smaller pieces called chunks.
This helps the system retrieve only the most relevant information.
Example chunk:
MelTech Computers sells HP EliteBook 840 G6 for GHS 4,200.
The laptop has Core i5 processor, 8GB RAM, and 256GB SSD.
It is currently available and comes with 3 months warranty.
For final-year project, a good chunk size can be:
300 to 500 words per chunk
For product data, each product can be one chunk.
________________________________________
Step 4: Embedding Generation
Each chunk is converted into a numerical representation called an embedding.
An embedding helps the system understand meaning.
For example, these questions may have similar meaning:
Do you have HP laptops?
Are HP laptops available?
Can I buy an HP laptop from your shop?
The embedding model helps the system understand that these questions are related.
Possible embedding models:
all-MiniLM-L6-v2
bge-small-en
nomic-embed-text
text-embedding models
For your local final-year version, use:
Sentence Transformers: all-MiniLM-L6-v2
or, if using Ollama:
nomic-embed-text
________________________________________
Step 5: Vector Database Storage
The embeddings are stored in a vector database.
The vector database allows fast similarity search.
For final-year project, use:
ChromaDB
Alternative:
FAISS
The vector database will store:
chunk_id
business_id
chunk_text
embedding
source_type
created_at
Example:
business_id: 12
source_type: product
chunk_text: HP EliteBook 840 G6 costs GHS 4,200...
embedding: [0.023, -0.015, 0.781, ...]
The business_id is very important because each business must only search its own data.
________________________________________
Step 6: Customer Question Processing
When a customer asks:
Do you have HP laptops?
The system performs these actions:
1.	Detects which business the customer is chatting with.
2.	Converts the question into an embedding.
3.	Searches only that business’s vector data.
4.	Retrieves the most relevant chunks.
5.	Sends the retrieved context and customer question to the LLM.
________________________________________
Step 7: Similarity Search
The system compares the customer’s question with the stored business data.
It may retrieve the top 3 to 5 most relevant chunks.
Example retrieved chunks:
Chunk 1:
HP EliteBook 840 G6 is available for GHS 4,200.

Chunk 2:
HP Pavilion 15 is available for GHS 5,100.

Chunk 3:
HP ProBook 450 is available for GHS 3,900.
________________________________________
Step 8: LLM Response Generation
The retrieved information is passed to the AI model.
The prompt should instruct the AI clearly:
You are a customer support assistant for MelTech Computers.
Answer the customer's question using only the business information provided.
Do not invent prices, products, discounts, or availability.
If the answer is not found in the context, say you do not have that information and recommend contacting staff.
Be polite, brief, and helpful.
Then the system adds:
Business context:
[retrieved chunks]

Customer question:
Do you have HP laptops?
AI response:
Yes. We currently have three HP laptops available:
1. HP EliteBook 840 G6 - GHS 4,200
2. HP Pavilion 15 - GHS 5,100
3. HP ProBook 450 - GHS 3,900

Would you like the specifications for any of them?

11. AI Guardrails
The AI must follow rules so it does not mislead customers.
Guardrail 1: Answer only from business data
If the answer is not in the retrieved context:
I do not have that information yet. Please contact the business directly.
Guardrail 2: Do not invent prices
Wrong:
The laptop is GHS 5,000.
Correct:
I do not have the price for that product in the current business data.
Guardrail 3: Escalate sensitive questions
For pharmacies:
I cannot provide medical advice. Please speak to a licensed pharmacist.
Guardrail 4: Detect low confidence
If the similarity score is too low, the AI should not answer confidently.
Example:
I could not find that information in the business records. Would you like me to connect you to a staff member?
Guardrail 5: Human handoff
If customer says:
I want to speak to a human.
The system should create an escalation record.
________________________________________
12. AI Confidence Scoring
The system should calculate a basic confidence score using retrieval similarity.
Example:
Similarity score above 0.75 = confident
Similarity score between 0.50 and 0.75 = moderate
Similarity score below 0.50 = low confidence
Simple decision logic:
if similarity_score < 0.50:
    return "I do not have enough information about that. Please contact a staff member."
else:
    generate_ai_answer()
This helps reduce wrong answers.

14. Example Pseudocode
def handle_customer_message(business_id, customer_message):
    # Step 1: Check if customer wants human support
    if "human" in customer_message.lower() or "agent" in customer_message.lower():
        create_escalation(business_id, customer_message)
        return "I will connect you to a staff member."

    # Step 2: Convert customer question into embedding
    question_embedding = embedding_model.encode(customer_message)

    # Step 3: Retrieve relevant business information
    retrieved_chunks = vector_db.search(
        business_id=business_id,
        query_embedding=question_embedding,
        top_k=5
    )

    # Step 4: Check confidence
    if retrieved_chunks[0].score < 0.50:
        return "I do not have that information yet. Please contact the business directly."

    # Step 5: Build AI prompt
    prompt = build_prompt(
        context=retrieved_chunks,
        question=customer_message
    )

    # Step 6: Generate answer
    answer = llm.generate(prompt)

    # Step 7: Save chat history
    save_chat_message(business_id, customer_message, answer)

    return answer

19. Methodology Summary
The system will be developed using a modular architecture made up of a frontend dashboard, backend API, relational database, vector database, and AI RAG engine. Business owners will upload products, services, prices, and FAQs through the dashboard. The backend will clean and convert this information into text chunks, generate embeddings, and store them in a vector database. When a customer asks a question, the system will retrieve the most relevant business information and pass it to a language model to generate a response. The AI will be restricted to the business’s own data to reduce hallucination. The final response will be delivered through a web chat interface, with future support for WhatsApp and voice interaction.
