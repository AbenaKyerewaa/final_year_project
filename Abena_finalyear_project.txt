# **EasyBiz AI: An AI Customer Support Assistant for Local Businesses in Ghana**

 1. The Big Vision
Many local businesses in Ghana are growing through WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, phone calls, and walk-in customers. But most of them do not have dedicated customer service staff.

A customer may ask:
> “Do you have HP laptops?”  
> “How much is your delivery?”  
> “Are you open on Sunday?”  
> “Can I pay with MoMo?”  
> “Do you have this drug?”  
> “What are your school fees?”  
> “What time is church service?”

Most business owners answer these questions manually every day. This wastes time, delays customers, and sometimes causes them to lose sales.

EasyBiz AI solves this by giving every small business its own AI assistant that can answer customer questions automatically using the business’s own information.**

The business owner does not need to know coding. They simply upload their products, prices, services, FAQs, opening hours, location, and contact details. The AI then uses that information to respond to customers through WhatsApp, web chat, and voice.

---

 2. The Problem
Small and medium businesses in Ghana face serious customer communication challenges.
Many of them:
- Do not have money to hire customer support staff.
- Receive repeated questions every day.
- Lose customers because replies are slow.
- Use WhatsApp as their main business platform but reply manually.
- Have product and price information scattered in notebooks, flyers, Excel sheets, WhatsApp chats, or memory.
- Cannot operate 24/7.
- Struggle to serve customers who prefer voice or local languages.

This affects shops, churches, pharmacies, restaurants, schools, clinics, salons, logistics businesses, and many others.

The problem is not that these businesses do not have customers.  
The problem is that they cannot respond quickly, consistently, and professionally.




3. The Proposed Solution
 **EasyBiz AI**
EasyBiz AI is an AI-powered assistant platform that helps local businesses automatically respond to customer enquiries.
The business owner creates an account and enters business information such as:

- Opening hours

Another example for a church:

5. The AI Features
 A. RAG-Based Answering System
This is the main AI feature.
RAG means **Retrieval-Augmented Generation**.

Instead of the AI guessing answers, it first searches the business’s uploaded information, then uses that information to generate a reply.

For example, if a laptop shop uploads this:
| Product | Price | Status |
| HP EliteBook 840 G6 | GHS 4,200 | Available |
| HP Pavilion 15 | GHS 5,100 | Available |
| Dell Latitude 5400 | GHS 3,800 | Out of stock |

If a customer asks:
> Do you have HP laptops?
The AI searches the uploaded product list and answers only based on that data.
This reduces hallucination and makes the system more trustworthy.


B. WhatsApp Integration
WhatsApp is very important because many Ghanaian businesses already use WhatsApp for selling.
The AI should connect to WhatsApp Business so that customers can chat with the business normally.
The business owner does not need to sit online all day.

The AI can respond to:
- Product questions
- Price questions
- Location questions
- FAQs
- Delivery questions
- Order enquiries

Example:
> Customer: How much is iPhone 12?  
> AI: The iPhone 12 is currently GHS 3,900. It is available in black and blue. Would you like to reserve one?

C. Web Chat Widget
The business can also add the AI assistant to its website.
A small chat button can appear at the bottom of the website:
> “Chat with us”
This is useful for schools, churches, restaurants, clinics, and larger shops.


D. Voice Support
Some customers prefer speaking instead of typing.
Voice support can allow a customer to speak a question, and the system converts the voice to text.

Example:
Customer speaks:
> “Do you sell rice cookers?”
The system converts it to text, searches the business data, and responds.
Later, the system can also reply with voice.

This is very powerful for Ghana because some customers may not type well, but they can speak comfortably.

 E. Local Language Support
A Ghana-focused version should support local languages gradually.
Start with English and simple Ghanaian English.
Later add:
- Twi
- Ga
- Ewe
- Hausa
- Fante
Example:
Customer:
> Mo, wo wɔ HP laptop?
AI:
> Aane, yɛwɔ HP laptop ahorow mmiɛnsa. Ne boɔ firi GHS 3,500.
This makes the project more locally relevant and stronger than a normal chatbot.

 F. Business Dashboard
The owner should have a simple dashboard where they can:
- Add products
- Edit prices
- Mark items as available or out of stock
- Upload FAQs
- View customer questions
- See unanswered questions
- See popular products
- Update opening hours
- Pause or activate the AI

This dashboard is important because the AI is only as useful as the information the business gives it.

 G. Human Handoff
The AI should know when to stop and send the customer to a human.
For example:
- The question is too complicated.
- The customer wants to negotiate.
- The customer wants a custom order.
- The AI is not sure.
- The customer is angry.
- A pharmacy customer asks for medical advice.
- A school applicant has a special admission case.

Example:
> I may need a staff member to help with this. Please hold on while I forward your request.
This makes the system safer and more professional.

 12. RAG Flow
The RAG process should work like this:
1. Business owner uploads product list or FAQ.
2. System cleans the data.
3. System splits the information into small chunks.
4. Each chunk is converted into embeddings.
5. Embeddings are stored in a vector database.
6. Customer asks a question.
7. System converts the question into an embedding.
8. Vector database retrieves the most relevant business information.
9. The LLM generates an answer using only the retrieved data.
10. The answer is sent back to the customer.

Example:
```text
Customer question:
"Do you have HP laptops?"
Retrieved data:
- HP EliteBook 840 G6, GHS 4,200, Available
- HP Pavilion 15, GHS 5,100, Available
- HP ProBook 450, GHS 3,900, Available

AI answer:
"Yes, we currently have three HP laptops available..."
```

13. Safety Rules for the AI
The AI should follow strict rules.
It should not:
- Invent prices.
- Claim a product is available when it is not in the database.
- Give medical diagnosis.
- Give legal advice.
- Promise delivery times unless they are in the business data.
- Share private business information.
- Insult customers.
- Continue arguing with angry customers.

It should say:
> I do not have that information yet. Please contact a staff member for help.
This makes the system more reliable.

21. Evaluation Metrics
For the final-year project, evaluate the system using:
1. Response Accuracy
Check whether the AI gives correct answers based on uploaded business data.

2. Retrieval Accuracy
Check whether the RAG system retrieves the right information.

3. Response Time
Measure how long it takes to answer a customer question.

4. Hallucination Rate
Check how often the AI gives answers not found in the business data.

5. User Satisfaction
Let business owners or students test the system and rate it.

6. Ease of Use
Check whether a non-technical business owner can upload data and use the dashboard.
